# Ambassador Programs - Sharp Edges

## Ambassador Scandal

### **Id**
ambassador-scandal
### **Summary**
Ambassador does something that reflects poorly on brand
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Public ambassador behavior damages reputation
### **Why**
  Ambassadors represent your brand.
  Their actions are associated with you.
  Viral moments spread fast.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Managing Ambassador Risk
  
  ### Prevention
  - Clear code of conduct
  - Social media guidelines
  - Regular check-ins
  - Values alignment in recruitment
  
  ### Response Protocol
  1. Assess situation quickly
  2. Private conversation first
  3. If public: Acknowledge you're reviewing
  4. Fair investigation
  5. Proportional action
  6. Learn and update policies
  
  ### Levels of Response
  | Severity | Action |
  |----------|--------|
  | Minor | Private feedback |
  | Moderate | Warning, coaching |
  | Serious | Suspension pending review |
  | Severe | Immediate removal |
  
  ### After Removal
  - Brief public statement if needed
  - No public attacks
  - Update recruitment process
  - Review similar situations
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Ambassador post goes viral negatively
  - Community complaints about ambassador
  - Media reaching out
  - Brand association damage
### **Detection Pattern**
ambassador|representative|scandal|controversy

## Ambassador Churn

### **Id**
ambassador-churn
### **Summary**
High turnover in ambassador program
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Ambassadors leaving faster than joining
### **Why**
  Not enough value for effort.
  Better opportunities elsewhere.
  Burnout.
  Lost interest in product.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Reducing Ambassador Churn
  
  ### Exit Interviews
  - Always conduct when ambassadors leave
  - Anonymous survey option
  - Look for patterns
  
  ### Common Reasons and Fixes
  | Reason | Fix |
  |--------|-----|
  | Not enough value | Increase rewards, access |
  | Too much work | Reduce requirements |
  | No recognition | More public appreciation |
  | Product issues | Feed back to product team |
  | Life changes | Graceful alumni status |
  
  ### Retention Tactics
  - Regular engagement
  - Clear progression path
  - Meaningful rewards
  - Community among ambassadors
  - Direct access to team
  
  ### Healthy Churn
  - Some churn is natural
  - Target: <30% annual turnover
  - Replace quickly
  - Alumni network
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Ambassadors going inactive
  - Not hitting activity minimums
  - Resignations increasing
  - Difficulty recruiting
### **Detection Pattern**
leaving|inactive|stepping down|not enough time

## Ambassador Cliques

### **Id**
ambassador-cliques
### **Summary**
Ambassador program becomes exclusive club
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Existing ambassadors block new voices
### **Why**
  Long-term ambassadors territorial.
  Program becomes insider club.
  New perspectives blocked.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Preventing Ambassador Cliques
  
  ### Program Structure
  - Regular cohorts (quarterly intake)
  - Term limits with re-application
  - Diverse recruitment
  - Cross-cohort activities
  
  ### Culture Building
  - Mentorship (senior → new)
  - Collaborative projects
  - Shared recognition
  - Inclusive communication
  
  ### Governance
  - Clear rules for all
  - No unofficial hierarchies
  - Ambassador feedback council
  - Staff oversight
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Same people dominate
  - New ambassadors feel excluded
  - Resistance to new members
  - We've always done it this way
### **Detection Pattern**
exclusive|clique|left out|inner circle

## Ambassador Competitors

### **Id**
ambassador-competitors
### **Summary**
Ambassadors secretly working with competitors
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Ambassador promoting or working for competition
### **Why**
  Conflict of interest.
  Insider access being shared.
  Divided loyalty.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Conflict of Interest Management
  
  ### Clear Policy
  - Define competing products
  - Disclosure requirements
  - Exclusivity expectations
  - Review process for conflicts
  
  ### Application Screening
  - Ask about other affiliations
  - Check public profiles
  - Reference checks
  - NDA for sensitive access
  
  ### Discovery Protocol
  1. Confirm the situation
  2. Private conversation
  3. Assess intent and impact
  4. Decision: allow, modify, or remove
  5. Update policies if needed
  
  ### Nuance
  - Multi-product ambassadors can be OK
  - Context matters
  - Don't overreact
  - Fairness in decisions
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Ambassador promoting competitor
  - Reduced enthusiasm
  - Access misuse
  - Community reports
### **Detection Pattern**
competitor|conflict of interest|also works|promoting
