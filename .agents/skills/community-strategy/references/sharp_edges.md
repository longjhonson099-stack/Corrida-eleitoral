# Community Strategy - Sharp Edges

## Community Without Audience

### **Id**
community-without-audience
### **Summary**
Building community before having any audience
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Launching a community platform with zero existing followers
### **Why**
  No seed members means no initial activity.
  Empty channels feel dead and repel visitors.
  Catch-22: people join active communities, but need people to be active.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Build Audience First
  
  ### The Sequence
  ```
  1. Build audience (newsletter, Twitter, content)
  2. Identify most engaged members
  3. Invite them to private community
  4. Get to 50-100 active members
  5. THEN open to public
  ```
  
  ### Seed Member Criteria
  - Already engaging with your content
  - Will show up and participate
  - Aligned with community values
  - Mix of personalities (not all lurkers)
  
  ### Minimum Viable Activity
  | Before Launch | Target |
  |---------------|--------|
  | Committed seed members | 50-100 |
  | Daily active posters | 10+ |
  | Content in channels | 3 days worth |
  | Welcome messages ready | Yes |
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Ghost town community
  - Only founders posting
  - New members leave immediately
  - "Is anyone here?" messages
### **Detection Pattern**
empty community|no activity|ghost town|dead server

## Community Strategy Mismatch

### **Id**
community-strategy-mismatch
### **Summary**
Community strategy doesn't match business model
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Community goals conflict with how the business makes money
### **Why**
  If community success hurts revenue, it will be deprioritized.
  Misaligned incentives create tension.
  Community team fights uphill battle.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Aligning Community with Business
  
  ### Alignment Canvas
  | Business Goal | Community Contribution |
  |---------------|----------------------|
  | Reduce churn | Members help each other |
  | Reduce support costs | Peer-to-peer support |
  | Increase referrals | Advocates bring friends |
  | Product feedback | Direct user insights |
  | Brand awareness | Member-generated content |
  
  ### Red Flags of Misalignment
  - Community budget is first to be cut
  - No exec sponsor for community
  - Community metrics not in company OKRs
  - Community seen as "nice to have"
  
  ### Fix Strategies
  1. Tie community metrics to business metrics
  2. Get executive sponsor
  3. Report on $ impact, not just engagement
  4. Make community essential to product experience
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Community budget cut
  - No executive support
  - Team of one, overwhelmed
  - Community is afterthought
### **Detection Pattern**
budget cut|no support|deprioritized|why do we have community

## Culture Drift

### **Id**
culture-drift
### **Summary**
Community culture drifts from original values
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
As community grows, original culture gets diluted or changes
### **Why**
  New members outnumber old members.
  Values weren't documented or reinforced.
  Growth prioritized over culture fit.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Preventing Culture Drift
  
  ### Documentation
  - Write down values explicitly
  - Document "what we do" and "what we don't do"
  - Create onboarding that teaches culture
  - Tell origin stories regularly
  
  ### Reinforcement
  | Mechanism | Frequency |
  |-----------|-----------|
  | Value-aligned recognition | Daily |
  | Calling out anti-norms | As needed |
  | Culture-focused AMAs | Monthly |
  | Values retrospective | Quarterly |
  
  ### Scaling Culture
  - Train moderators as culture carriers
  - Create ambassador program for values
  - New member mentorship program
  - Sub-community culture alignment checks
  
### **Symptoms**
  - This place has changed
  - OG members leaving
  - Toxicity increasing
  - Values feel hollow
### **Detection Pattern**
community changed|not like before|lost its soul|toxic

## Governance Vacuum

### **Id**
governance-vacuum
### **Summary**
No clear decision-making process as community scales
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Community outgrows founder-led governance but nothing replaces it
### **Why**
  Founder can't personally handle everything.
  Decisions stall or become inconsistent.
  Members frustrated by lack of clarity.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Governance Transition
  
  ### Signs You Need More Structure
  - Same questions asked repeatedly
  - Decisions taking too long
  - Inconsistent rule enforcement
  - Members unsure who to ask
  
  ### Transition Steps
  1. Document current implicit rules
  2. Create mod team with clear authority
  3. Establish escalation paths
  4. Set up regular governance reviews
  5. Create transparency mechanisms
  
  ### Governance Artifacts
  - Community guidelines (public)
  - Mod handbook (internal)
  - Decision log (transparency)
  - Appeals process (fairness)
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Who decides this?
  - Inconsistent enforcement
  - Founder bottleneck
  - Member frustration
### **Detection Pattern**
who decides|unfair|inconsistent|no process

## Vanity Metrics Trap

### **Id**
vanity-metrics-trap
### **Summary**
Optimizing for wrong metrics
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Chasing member count instead of community health
### **Why**
  Easy to measure doesn't mean important.
  Growth can mask declining health.
  Wrong metrics drive wrong behavior.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Right Metrics Framework
  
  ### Vanity vs. Value Metrics
  | Vanity (Avoid) | Value (Track) |
  |----------------|---------------|
  | Total members | Active members (DAU/MAU) |
  | Messages sent | Conversations (threads) |
  | Page views | Time to first value |
  | Followers | Referral rate |
  | Likes | Help requests resolved |
  
  ### Health Indicators
  - Retention: Do members come back?
  - Depth: Are connections forming?
  - Contribution: Is value being created?
  - Sentiment: Do members feel good?
  - Referral: Do members invite others?
  
  ### Reporting Structure
  1. Weekly: Activity metrics
  2. Monthly: Health metrics
  3. Quarterly: Outcome metrics
  4. Annually: Strategic metrics
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Big numbers, low engagement
  - Growth but declining activity
  - Can't prove business value
  - Leadership skeptical
### **Detection Pattern**
numbers look good but|not engaged|low retention|vanity
