# Creative Communications - Sharp Edges

## Vague Brief

### **Id**
vague-brief
### **Summary**
Briefing creative with unclear objectives
### **Severity**
critical
### **Situation**
  "We need something cool for social." "Make it pop."
  No target audience, no goal, no success criteria.
  
### **Why**
  Creatives produce work that misses the mark. Multiple rounds of revisions
  trying to hit an undefined target. Time and money wasted.
  Tension between stakeholders and creative team.
  
### **Solution**
  Every brief must include:
  □ Target audience (who are we speaking to?)
  □ Objective (what do we want them to do?)
  □ Key message (what's the one thing?)
  □ Success criteria (how do we know it worked?)
  □ Constraints (budget, timeline, mandatories)
  
  No exceptions. No briefs without these elements.
  
### **Symptoms**
  - I'll know it when I see it
  - Endless revision cycles
  - Creative misses the mark
  - Stakeholder frustration
### **Detection Pattern**


## Revision Spiral

### **Id**
revision-spiral
### **Summary**
Endless rounds of changes without clear decision-maker
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
  Each stakeholder adds feedback. Scope creeps with each round.
  "One more small change" becomes ten more changes.
  
### **Why**
  Deadlines missed. Creative quality degrades with over-revision.
  Team burns out. Costs spiral. Final product is a camel designed by committee.
  
### **Solution**
  - Defined approval process with clear decision-maker
  - Maximum revision rounds specified upfront
  - Consolidated feedback (one voice to creative)
  - Time-boxed review periods
  
  # Revision structure:
  Round 1: Major feedback
  Round 2: Refinements
  Round 3: Final tweaks only
  No Round 4
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Dozens of revision rounds
  - No clear approver
  - Deadlines slipping
  - Team burnout
### **Detection Pattern**


## Off Brand Asset

### **Id**
off-brand-asset
### **Summary**
Creating assets that don't follow brand guidelines
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
  Wrong logo usage, off-palette colors, inconsistent typography.
  Each piece looks like a different company.
  
### **Why**
  Erodes brand recognition. Looks unprofessional.
  Undermines all the equity built in brand. Confuses customers.
  
### **Solution**
  - Accessible, clear brand guidelines
  - Brand review as mandatory checkpoint
  - Templates for common asset types
  - Brand training for all creative contributors
  
  # Brand review checklist:
  □ Logo usage correct
  □ Colors match palette
  □ Typography follows hierarchy
  □ Tone matches voice guidelines
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Inconsistent look across assets
  - Brand police constantly fixing
  - External vendors go off-brand
  - No brand recognition
### **Detection Pattern**


## Platform Mismatch

### **Id**
platform-mismatch
### **Summary**
Creating one asset and forcing it into every platform
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
  TV spot cropped for Instagram Stories. Print ad repurposed for Twitter.
  Same asset everywhere without adaptation.
  
### **Why**
  Cropped visuals that miss key elements. Wrong aspect ratios.
  Text too small to read. Captions cut off. Looks lazy and unprofessional.
  
### **Solution**
  - Design for each platform from the start
  - Lead platform first, then adapt (don't just resize)
  - Understand platform constraints before creating
  - Platform-specific asset checklists
  
  # Platform considerations:
  - Aspect ratios
  - Text safe zones
  - Video length limits
  - Audio requirements
  - Interactive elements
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Cropped/stretched assets
  - Text cut off or unreadable
  - Videos wrong length
  - Assets look amateur
### **Detection Pattern**


## Uncleared Asset

### **Id**
uncleared-asset
### **Summary**
Using photos, music, fonts without proper licensing
### **Severity**
critical
### **Situation**
  Stock photos used beyond license scope. Music in video without sync rights.
  Downloaded from Google Images. Used "temp" music and shipped it.
  
### **Why**
  Legal exposure. Takedown notices. Lawsuits from rights holders.
  Embarrassing removal of published content. Fines.
  
### **Solution**
  - Clear rights for everything before production
  - Maintain asset license database
  - Never use "temp" assets in final work
  - When in doubt, don't use it
  
  # License checklist:
  □ Commercial use allowed
  □ Scope covers intended use
  □ Duration covers campaign
  □ Territory covers markets
  □ License documented and stored
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Takedown notices
  - Legal letters from rights holders
  - Emergency asset replacements
  - Unknown license status
### **Detection Pattern**


## Approval Bottleneck

### **Id**
approval-bottleneck
### **Summary**
Creative sitting in approval queue while deadlines pass
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
  Key approver unavailable. No escalation path. Creative blocked for weeks.
  Deadline missed while waiting for approval.
  
### **Why**
  Missed deadlines. Campaigns delayed. Creative teams blocked.
  Last-minute rushing when approval finally comes.
  
### **Solution**
  - Defined SLAs for approval
  - Backup approvers for each role
  - Escalation process
  - Approval timeboxes
  - Tools that surface approval needs prominently
  
  # Approval structure:
  - 24-hour SLA for minor assets
  - 48-hour SLA for major assets
  - Backup approver if primary unavailable
  - Auto-escalation after SLA
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Assets stuck in review
  - "Waiting for approval" status
  - Missed deadlines
  - Approvers overloaded
### **Detection Pattern**


## Single Use Asset

### **Id**
single-use-asset
### **Summary**
Creating expensive creative that can only be used once
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
  Highly specific headlines, dated references, single-platform optimization.
  Expensive creative with no reuse potential.
  
### **Why**
  High cost per use. Constantly creating from scratch.
  No asset library to leverage. Inefficient creative operations.
  
### **Solution**
  - Design for modularity where possible
  - Create master assets that can be adapted
  - Build asset library
  - Consider longevity in brief
  
  # Reusability checklist:
  □ Can this be adapted for other platforms?
  □ Is the message evergreen or dated?
  □ Can elements be reused?
  □ Are we building library assets?
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Starting from scratch each time
  - No asset library
  - High creative costs
  - Duplicate effort across campaigns
### **Detection Pattern**


## Literal Interpretation

### **Id**
literal-interpretation
### **Summary**
Creative that just illustrates the product literally
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
  Showing a laptop to sell software. Stock photos of "business people."
  Just depicting the product, not the benefit or emotion.
  
### **Why**
  Invisible in crowded markets. Doesn't differentiate.
  Boring. Fails to connect emotionally. No memorability.
  
### **Solution**
  - Brief for emotion and differentiation, not just depiction
  - Push for concepts that surprise
  - Show the benefit, not just the product
  - Take creative risks worth taking
  
  # Creative direction:
  Literal: "Show people using our software"
  Better: "Show the feeling of finally being organized"
  
  Show outcomes, not just products
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Interchangeable with competitors
  - Boring, forgettable creative
  - No emotional connection
  - Safe but invisible
### **Detection Pattern**


## Unfeedbackable Feedback

### **Id**
unfeedbackable-feedback
### **Summary**
Giving vague feedback creative can't act on
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
  "Make it more premium." "It needs more energy."
  "I'll know it when I see it." Subjective, vague, unactionable.
  
### **Why**
  Endless rounds trying to guess what's wanted.
  Frustrated creative team. Wasted time. Relationship damage.
  
### **Solution**
  - Train stakeholders on giving actionable feedback
  - "Instead of X, try Y because Z"
  - Reference examples
  - Tie feedback to brief objectives
  
  # Actionable feedback format:
  Not: "Make it pop more"
  But: "The headline doesn't stand out. Can we increase contrast
        or make the font bolder so it reads faster?"
  
  Always: "How does this fail to achieve the stated goal?"
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Creative can't act on feedback
  - Multiple interpretations of notes
  - Guessing what stakeholder wants
  - Revision roulette
### **Detection Pattern**


## Over Produced Asset

### **Id**
over-produced-asset
### **Summary**
Creating expensive content when low-fi would work better
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
  Agency-quality video for organic social. Studio photography for Stories.
  High production value where authenticity wins.
  
### **Why**
  Slow to produce. Expensive. Often outperformed by authentic, lower-production
  content. Wrong fit for platform.
  
### **Solution**
  - Match production level to platform and purpose
  - Test lo-fi before committing to hi-fi
  - Embrace authenticity where appropriate
  - Production value should serve strategy, not ego
  
  # Production matching:
  Brand campaign: High production
  Organic social: Authentic, lower production
  Ads: Depends on platform
  Stories/TikTok: Often lo-fi wins
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Lo-fi content outperforms expensive content
  - Slow production cycles
  - High costs for low-engagement platforms
  - Over-polished for context
### **Detection Pattern**


## Orphan Asset

### **Id**
orphan-asset
### **Summary**
Creating great creative that never gets distributed
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
  Perfect video sitting on hard drives. Assets created but never launched.
  Campaign killed. Strategy changed. Approval never came.
  
### **Why**
  Complete waste of resources. Demoralized creative team. Sunk costs.
  
### **Solution**
  - Distribution planning before production
  - Launch commitment before investment
  - Kill projects early if strategy changes
  - Better to produce less that ships than more that doesn't
  
  # Pre-production checkpoint:
  □ Distribution channels confirmed
  □ Launch date committed
  □ Approvers identified and available
  □ Budget approved end-to-end
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Assets never published
  - Drives full of unused work
  - Strategy changes after production
  - Creative team demoralized
### **Detection Pattern**


## Translation Disaster

### **Id**
translation-disaster
### **Summary**
Localizing creative poorly without cultural context
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
  Literal translations that miss cultural context. Imagery that doesn't resonate
  or offends. Messages that don't land in new markets.
  
### **Why**
  Messages that don't land. Cultural insensitivity.
  Brand damage in new markets. Wasted localization investment.
  
### **Solution**
  - Cultural review alongside translation
  - Local market experts involved early
  - Test with target market audiences
  - Some concepts don't translate—create new ones
  
  # Localization checklist:
  □ Meaning preserved, not just words
  □ Cultural context considered
  □ Imagery appropriate for market
  □ Local expert reviewed
  □ In-market testing conducted
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Awkward translations
  - Cultural missteps
  - Poor performance in new markets
  - Negative local reception
### **Detection Pattern**
