# Community Growth

## Patterns


---
  #### **Name**
Acquisition Funnel
  #### **Description**
Structured approach to member acquisition
  #### **When To Use**
When designing growth strategy
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Community Acquisition Funnel
    
    ### Channels by Effectiveness
    | Channel | Quality | Volume | Cost |
    |---------|---------|--------|------|
    | Member referrals | Highest | Medium | Low |
    | Content/SEO | High | High | Medium |
    | Twitter/social | Medium | High | Low |
    | Paid ads | Variable | High | High |
    | Partnerships | High | Medium | Medium |
    
    ### Referral Loop Design
    ```
    MEMBER → SHARES VALUE → FRIEND SEES → JOINS → GETS VALUE → SHARES
    ```
    
    Key elements:
    - Make sharing easy (one-click invites)
    - Incentivize but don't bribe
    - Create shareable moments
    - Track referral source

Engagement Programs
  #### **Description**
Structured programs that drive participation
  #### **When To Use**
When building consistent engagement
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Engagement Program Types
    
    ### Daily Rituals
    - GM threads
    - Daily questions
    - Standup posts
    - Win sharing
    
    ### Weekly Programs
    | Day | Program | Format |
    |-----|---------|--------|
    | Monday | Week kickoff | Goals thread |
    | Wednesday | Office hours | Live Q&A |
    | Friday | Wins & learnings | Celebration |
    
    ### Monthly Initiatives
    - AMAs with guests
    - Challenges/competitions
    - Community projects
    - Retrospectives
    
    ### Gamification Elements
    - XP/points systems
    - Levels and roles
    - Badges and achievements
    - Leaderboards (careful!)
    

---
  #### **Name**
Event Playbook
  #### **Description**
Running community events that engage
  #### **When To Use**
When planning community events
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Event Types
    
    ### Virtual Events
    | Type | Platform | Best For |
    |------|----------|----------|
    | AMA | Discord Stage/Twitter Space | Expert access |
    | Workshop | Zoom/Discord | Learning |
    | Social | Discord VC | Bonding |
    | Demo Day | YouTube Live | Showcasing |
    
    ### Event Checklist
    **Before (1 week)**
    - [ ] Guest confirmed
    - [ ] Promotion started
    - [ ] Tech tested
    - [ ] Questions collected
    
    **Day of**
    - [ ] Reminder sent
    - [ ] Recording ready
    - [ ] Mod coverage
    - [ ] Backup plan
    
    **After (48h)**
    - [ ] Recording shared
    - [ ] Highlights posted
    - [ ] Follow-up thread
    - [ ] Feedback collected
    

---
  #### **Name**
Content Strategy
  #### **Description**
Community-generated and curated content
  #### **When To Use**
When developing content approach
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Community Content Mix
    
    ### Content Sources
    | Source | Type | Frequency |
    |--------|------|-----------|
    | Team | Announcements, education | 2-3x/week |
    | Members | Questions, projects, wins | Daily |
    | Curated | Industry news, resources | Daily |
    | UGC | Member spotlights, stories | Weekly |
    
    ### Newsletter Framework
    - Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly
    - Content: Community highlights, top posts, events
    - Personal: Include member voices
    - CTA: Drive back to community
    
    ### Encouraging UGC
    - Prompt sharing (templates, questions)
    - Recognize contributions publicly
    - Feature best content
    - Make creation easy
    

## Anti-Patterns


---
  #### **Name**
Growth Hacking Community
  #### **Description**
Using aggressive tactics to pump numbers
  #### **Why Bad**
    Attracts wrong members. Damages culture.
    Vanity metrics, no real engagement.
    Burns trust for temporary gains.
    
  #### **What To Do Instead**
    Focus on member value first.
    Grow through quality, not tricks.
    Measure engagement, not just size.
    

---
  #### **Name**
Event Burnout
  #### **Description**
Too many events, too little impact
  #### **Why Bad**
    Team exhausted. Members fatigued.
    Each event matters less. Quality drops.
    Unsustainable pace.
    
  #### **What To Do Instead**
    Fewer, higher-quality events.
    Build anticipation through scarcity.
    Measure event ROI, cut low performers.
    

---
  #### **Name**
Engagement Farming
  #### **Description**
Artificial engagement tactics
  #### **Why Bad**
    Members see through it.
    Shallow interactions, no real value.
    Metrics look good, community feels empty.
    
  #### **What To Do Instead**
    Create genuine reasons to engage.
    Value-driven interaction.
    Quality conversations over quantity.
