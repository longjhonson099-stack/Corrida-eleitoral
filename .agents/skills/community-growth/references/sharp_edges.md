# Community Growth - Sharp Edges

## Growth Over Health

### **Id**
growth-over-health
### **Summary**
Prioritizing growth metrics over community health
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Pushing for member count while engagement declines
### **Why**
  Leadership wants big numbers.
  Easy to measure growth.
  Health metrics harder to track.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Balancing Growth and Health
  
  ### Health-First Metrics
  | Track This | Not Just This |
  |------------|---------------|
  | DAU/MAU ratio | Total members |
  | Messages per member | Total messages |
  | Retention rate | New joins |
  | NPS/satisfaction | Page views |
  
  ### Growth Gates
  Only scale when:
  - 40%+ DAU/MAU ratio
  - 50%+ week-1 retention
  - Positive member sentiment
  - Ops can handle volume
  
  ### Healthy Growth Rate
  - 10-20% monthly is sustainable
  - 50%+ monthly risks culture dilution
  - Quality always beats quantity
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Big numbers, low engagement
  - "Ghost town" feeling
  - OG members leaving
  - Culture complaints
### **Detection Pattern**
growing but|numbers up|engagement down|quiet

## Event Fatigue

### **Id**
event-fatigue
### **Summary**
Members tired of constant events
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Event attendance declining despite more events
### **Why**
  Team thinks more events = more engagement.
  No event strategy, just calendar filling.
  Same formats, no innovation.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Preventing Event Fatigue
  
  ### Event Audit
  | Question | Action |
  |----------|--------|
  | < 20% of expected attendance? | Cut or redesign |
  | Declining over time? | Evaluate format |
  | No buzz after? | Wrong content |
  | Team exhausted? | Reduce frequency |
  
  ### Quality Over Quantity
  - Max 2-3 events per month
  - Each event has clear purpose
  - Build anticipation (not routine)
  - Rotate formats
  
  ### Event ROI
  - Track attendance trends
  - Post-event engagement
  - Member feedback
  - Team effort vs. impact
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Declining attendance
  - Another event?
  - Team burnout
  - Low post-event engagement
### **Detection Pattern**
too many events|event fatigue|nobody showed|burnout

## Referral Spam

### **Id**
referral-spam
### **Summary**
Incentivized referrals attracting low-quality members
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Referral program brings volume but wrong people
### **Why**
  Rewards too generous.
  No quality filter.
  Incentivizing wrong behavior.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Quality Referral Design
  
  ### Anti-Gaming Measures
  - Reward after referred member is active (not just joined)
  - Cap referrals per person
  - Quality threshold (X messages, Y days active)
  - Manual review for top referrers
  
  ### Right Incentives
  | Bad | Good |
  |-----|------|
  | Cash per referral | Recognition and access |
  | Unlimited rewards | Tiered, capped benefits |
  | Instant payout | Delayed after activation |
  
  ### Referral Quality Tracking
  - Referral vs organic retention
  - Activity of referred members
  - Source quality scoring
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Spam invites
  - Referred members inactive
  - Gaming the system
  - Quality decline
### **Detection Pattern**
referral spam|gaming|low quality|inactive referrals

## Content Vacuum

### **Id**
content-vacuum
### **Summary**
Community has no content or all content from team
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Members consume but don't create
### **Why**
  No prompts or encouragement.
  Too high barrier to post.
  Fear of judgment.
  Team dominates conversation.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Encouraging Member Content
  
  ### Lower the Bar
  - Simple prompts (yes/no, this or that)
  - Templates and frameworks
  - Celebrate all contributions
  - Private sharing options first
  
  ### Content Prompts
  - "What are you working on?"
  - "Share a win from this week"
  - "What's one thing you learned?"
  - "Show us your setup"
  
  ### Recognition
  - Feature member content
  - Member spotlight series
  - React to all posts
  - Thank contributors publicly
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Only team posting
  - Questions go unanswered by members
  - Low original content
  - Consumption-only behavior
### **Detection Pattern**
no one posts|only team|lurkers|quiet
