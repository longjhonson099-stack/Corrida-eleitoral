# Education Business - Sharp Edges

## Launch Flop

### **Id**
launch-flop
### **Summary**
Course launch generates minimal sales
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Launch week ends with disappointing revenue
### **Why**
  Audience too small.
  Positioning unclear.
  Insufficient launch runway.
  Price mismatch.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Recovering from a Launch Flop
  
  ### Immediate Assessment
  | Metric | What It Tells You |
  |--------|-------------------|
  | Email opens | Audience engagement |
  | Sales page visits | Interest level |
  | Cart abandons | Pricing/offer issues |
  | Actual sales | Market-message fit |
  
  ### Common Causes + Fixes
  | Cause | Fix |
  |-------|-----|
  | Small list | Grow list 3-6 months |
  | Cold audience | Nurture before next launch |
  | Unclear value | Rewrite positioning |
  | Too expensive | Validate or lower price |
  | Poor timing | Research audience calendar |
  
  ### Short-Term Recovery
  - Extend cart (don't panic discount)
  - Direct outreach to warm leads
  - Offer payment plan
  - Ask non-buyers why
  
  ### Long-Term Fix
  - Build email list intentionally
  - Warm up 4-6 weeks before launch
  - Validate with pre-sales next time
  - Get testimonials from small first cohort
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Single-digit sales
  - Low email engagement
  - Sales page bounces
  - Refund requests
### **Detection Pattern**
no sales|only sold|nobody bought|launch failed

## Refund Wave

### **Id**
refund-wave
### **Summary**
High refund rate after launch
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Significant percentage request refunds
### **Why**
  Overpromised in marketing.
  Course didn't match expectations.
  Wrong audience attracted.
  Buyer's remorse + easy refund.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Managing Refund Waves
  
  ### Healthy Refund Rates
  | Price Point | Acceptable Rate |
  |-------------|-----------------|
  | < $200 | 5-10% |
  | $200-1000 | 3-5% |
  | > $1000 | 1-3% |
  
  ### Diagnosis
  - Survey refund requesters
  - Look for patterns in reasons
  - Compare to marketing claims
  - Check sales page expectations
  
  ### Common Causes
  | Reason | Fix |
  |--------|-----|
  | "Not what I expected" | Clearer sales page |
  | "Too basic/advanced" | Better targeting |
  | "No time" | Pre-qualify commitment |
  | "Didn't deliver promise" | Fix course or marketing |
  
  ### Prevention
  - Show clear curriculum
  - Include "who this is NOT for"
  - Set realistic expectations
  - Deliver value fast (reduce remorse window)
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Refund requests spike
  - Complaints about mismatch
  - Negative reviews mention expectations
  - Not what I thought
### **Detection Pattern**
refund|not what I expected|want my money|disappointed

## Pricing Spiral

### **Id**
pricing-spiral
### **Summary**
Lowering price repeatedly, devaluing course
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Discounting more and more to get sales
### **Why**
  Panic after slow sales.
  Competing on price.
  Imposter syndrome.
  Not addressing real issue.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Escaping the Pricing Spiral
  
  ### Signs You're Spiraling
  - Regular deep discounts
  - "Sale" is permanent state
  - Price dropped multiple times
  - Competitors undercutting
  
  ### The Problem with Low Price
  - Attracts non-committed students
  - Lower completion rates
  - Harder to invest in quality
  - Can't afford to support students
  
  ### Recovery Path
  1. Stop discounting (cold turkey)
  2. Add value instead (bonuses)
  3. Create premium tier
  4. Reposition, don't re-price
  5. Gather testimonials at any price
  
  ### If Sales Are Slow
  | Instead of... | Do this... |
  |---------------|------------|
  | Lowering price | Add bonuses |
  | Permanent sale | Limited-time offer |
  | Competing on price | Differentiate on outcome |
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Price lower than last launch
  - Always on sale
  - Justifying with "it's accessible"
  - Competitors' prices driving yours
### **Detection Pattern**
lowered price|discount again|too expensive|cheaper

## No Recurring Revenue

### **Id**
no-recurring-revenue
### **Summary**
Income only from new course launches
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Revenue spikes at launch, nothing between
### **Why**
  No evergreen funnel.
  No subscription offering.
  No course ladder.
  Only one product.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Building Recurring Revenue
  
  ### Revenue Smoothing Options
  | Model | How |
  |-------|-----|
  | Evergreen funnel | Automated launch sequence |
  | Membership | Monthly/annual subscription |
  | Payment plans | Spread revenue over months |
  | Multiple products | Staggered launches |
  
  ### Evergreen Funnel Basics
  1. Webinar or video series (automated)
  2. Limited-time offer (perceived scarcity)
  3. Email sequence nurture
  4. Ongoing traffic (ads, content)
  
  ### Subscription Add-On
  - Community access ($X/month)
  - Office hours ($X/month)
  - Updated content ($X/year)
  
  ### The Goal
  - 50%+ revenue from non-launch sources
  - Predictable monthly baseline
  - Launches are bonuses, not survival
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Income only during launch weeks
  - Feast-famine cycle
  - Stress between launches
  - No predictable revenue
### **Detection Pattern**
only made money|between launches|need to launch|feast or famine
