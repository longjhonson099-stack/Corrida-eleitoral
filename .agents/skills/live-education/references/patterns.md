# Live Education

## Patterns


---
  #### **Name**
Cohort-Based Course Design
  #### **Description**
Structure for running live cohort courses
  #### **When To Use**
When creating a cohort-based course
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Cohort-Based Course Design
    
    ### Why Cohorts Win
    | Factor | Self-Paced | Cohort |
    |--------|------------|--------|
    | Completion | 5-15% | 60-85% |
    | Accountability | None | Peers + deadlines |
    | Networking | None | Built-in |
    | Price point | $50-500 | $500-5000+ |
    
    ### Cohort Structure
    ```
    Week 0: Orientation + community
    Week 1-N: Core content + live sessions
    Final Week: Capstone + celebration
    Post-cohort: Alumni community
    ```
    
    ### Weekly Rhythm
    | Day | Activity |
    |-----|----------|
    | Monday | New module unlocks |
    | Tue-Thu | Async work + community |
    | Friday | Live session |
    | Weekend | Catch up, project work |
    
    ### Live Session Format (90 min)
    1. Check-in / wins (10 min)
    2. Hot seat / Q&A (30 min)
    3. Teaching moment (20 min)
    4. Breakout exercise (20 min)
    5. Commitments for next week (10 min)
    
    ### Cohort Size Guidelines
    | Size | Vibe | Price |
    |------|------|-------|
    | 10-20 | Intimate, high-touch | Premium |
    | 20-50 | Balanced | Mid-tier |
    | 50-100 | Community energy | Lower per-seat |
    | 100+ | Event feel | Lowest per-seat |

  #### **Name**

  #### **Description**
Running effective live workshops
  #### **When To Use**
When designing or running workshops
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Workshop Facilitation
    
    ### Workshop vs Course
    - Workshop: Single session, specific outcome
    - Course: Multiple sessions, broader transformation
    
    ### Workshop Structure (2-3 hours)
    | Segment | Duration | Purpose |
    |---------|----------|---------|
    | Opening | 10 min | Energy, expectations |
    | Teaching | 20 min | Core concept |
    | Exercise 1 | 30 min | Apply learning |
    | Break | 10 min | Reset energy |
    | Teaching 2 | 15 min | Deeper layer |
    | Exercise 2 | 30 min | Build on first |
    | Share out | 20 min | Learn from peers |
    | Close | 15 min | Next steps, resources |
    
    ### Energy Management
    ```
    High energy → Teaching → Lower (absorbing)
    → Exercise (active) → Share (high) → Close
    ```
    
    ### Facilitation Techniques
    - Chat waterfall: "Type your answer, hit enter on 3"
    - Breakout rooms: 3-4 people, clear prompt
    - Polls: Keep engagement, gather data
    - Spotlight: Bring participants on camera
    
    ### The 10-Minute Rule
    - Never talk for more than 10 minutes
    - Always follow with interaction
    - If > 10 min, insert micro-engagement

  #### **Name**
Peer Learning Systems
  #### **Description**
Designing learning from each other
  #### **When To Use**
When building community-powered learning
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Peer Learning Systems
    
    ### Why Peer Learning
    - Students learn by teaching
    - Diverse perspectives
    - Scales instructor time
    - Builds relationships
    
    ### Peer Learning Structures
    | Structure | How It Works |
    |-----------|--------------|
    | Buddy pairs | 2 people, weekly check-ins |
    | Pods | 4-6 people, shared projects |
    | Peer review | Exchange and critique work |
    | Hot seats | One person, group feedback |
    | Mastermind | Rotating focus person |
    
    ### Buddy System
    - Match in Week 1
    - Weekly 30-min calls
    - Accountability partner
    - Progress check-ins
    
    ### Pod Design
    - 4-6 people per pod
    - Mix skill levels slightly
    - Assign pod leader
    - Shared Slack/Discord channel
    - Weekly pod meetings
    
    ### Peer Review Process
    1. Submit work by deadline
    2. Randomly assign 2 reviewers
    3. Provide structured feedback
    4. Review reviews (quality control)
    

---
  #### **Name**
Accountability Architecture
  #### **Description**
Building systems that ensure completion
  #### **When To Use**
When designing for high completion rates
  #### **Implementation**
    ## Accountability Architecture
    
    ### Accountability Layers
    | Layer | Mechanism |
    |-------|-----------|
    | Self | Clear deadlines, streaks |
    | Peer | Buddy, pod, public commits |
    | Instructor | Check-ins, progress tracking |
    | Stakes | Deposit, social pressure |
    
    ### Public Commitment
    - Week 1: Share your goal publicly
    - Weekly: Share progress in community
    - Final: Present to the group
    
    ### Progress Visibility
    - Leaderboards (optional, can demotivate)
    - Completion badges
    - Progress bar for cohort
    - Celebration of milestones
    
    ### Deadline Design
    | Type | Effect |
    |------|--------|
    | Soft | "Aim for Friday" | Low accountability |
    | Firm | "Due Friday" | Medium |
    | Hard | "Live review Saturday" | High |
    
    ### Recovery Paths
    - Don't punish falling behind
    - Office hours for catch-up
    - "Life happens" extensions
    - Focus on finishing, not perfection
    

## Anti-Patterns


---
  #### **Name**
Lecture-Only Live Sessions
  #### **Description**
Using live time for one-way content
  #### **Why Bad**
    Wastes live format.
    Could be async video.
    Boring, passive.
    No peer benefit.
    
  #### **What To Do Instead**
    Pre-record content.
    Use live for interaction.
    Q&A, hot seats, exercises.
    Leverage the group.
    

---
  #### **Name**
No Structure for Peer Connection
  #### **Description**
Expecting organic peer relationships
  #### **Why Bad**
    Connections don't form.
    Students feel isolated.
    Missed cohort value.
    
  #### **What To Do Instead**
    Assign buddies/pods.
    Structured icebreakers.
    Forced collaboration.
    Regular peer touchpoints.
    

---
  #### **Name**
Too Many Live Sessions
  #### **Description**
Overwhelming calendar with calls
  #### **Why Bad**
    Attendance drops.
    "Call fatigue" sets in.
    Feels like a job.
    
  #### **What To Do Instead**
    1-2 live sessions per week max.
    Make each one valuable.
    Record for async access.
    Optional vs required clarity.
