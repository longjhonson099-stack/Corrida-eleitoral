# Live Education - Sharp Edges

## Low Attendance

### **Id**
low-attendance
### **Summary**
Students stop showing up to live sessions
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Attendance drops after first few sessions
### **Why**
  Sessions not valuable enough.
  Wrong time for audience.
  Life gets in the way.
  Content could be async.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Fixing Low Attendance
  
  ### Make Sessions Unmissable
  - Don't repeat recorded content
  - Hot seats / live coaching
  - Guest experts
  - Peer activities
  - Real-time feedback on their work
  
  ### Optimal Scheduling
  | Audience | Best Times |
  |----------|------------|
  | US professionals | Tue-Thu 12pm or 7pm ET |
  | Global | Rotate times or offer 2 |
  | Parents | After 8pm or during school |
  
  ### Attendance Boosters
  - Calendar invites with reminders
  - Day-before email
  - Morning-of Slack ping
  - Attendance tracking visible
  - Bonus for perfect attendance
  
  ### If They Miss
  - Recordings available (but not too convenient)
  - Buddy shares notes
  - Quick catch-up option
  - Don't guilt-trip
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Attendance drops week over week
  - Same people always missing
  - Low energy in sessions
  - Students watching recordings instead
### **Detection Pattern**
couldn't make it|watched recording|missed|low attendance

## Silent Breakouts

### **Id**
silent-breakouts
### **Summary**
Breakout rooms are awkward and unproductive
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Breakout sessions fail to generate engagement
### **Why**
  Unclear instructions.
  No designated leader.
  Wrong group size.
  No accountability to share back.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Effective Breakout Rooms
  
  ### Setup Rules
  - 3-4 people max per room
  - Written instructions on screen
  - Designate a "reporter"
  - Time-box clearly (with warning)
  
  ### Instruction Template
  ```
  In your breakout (10 min):
  1. Each person shares X (2 min each)
  2. Pick one to share with main room
  3. Elect a reporter to present
  ```
  
  ### Facilitation
  - Pop into rooms to check
  - Broadcast time warnings
  - Have backup plan for stragglers
  - Celebrate share-outs
  
  ### If Still Struggling
  - Start with pairs, then groups
  - Use collaborative docs
  - Give example of "good" response
  - Make it about their real work
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Silent breakout rooms
  - We didn't know what to do
  - Same people always talking
  - Nothing to share back
### **Detection Pattern**
awkward|silent|didn't know|what were we supposed to

## Cohort Burnout

### **Id**
cohort-burnout
### **Summary**
Running cohorts is exhausting and unsustainable
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Instructor burns out after a few cohorts
### **Why**
  Too much live time.
  Answering same questions.
  No team support.
  Emotional labor.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Sustainable Cohort Operations
  
  ### Time Boundaries
  | Activity | Limit |
  |----------|-------|
  | Live sessions | 2-4 hrs/week |
  | Community | 30 min/day |
  | 1:1 coaching | Cap or eliminate |
  
  ### Leverage Others
  - Guest instructors
  - TA/community manager
  - Peer support structures
  - Alumni mentors
  
  ### Systematize Repetition
  - FAQ document
  - Recorded answers to common Qs
  - Templates for feedback
  - Standard operating procedures
  
  ### Recovery Between Cohorts
  - 2-4 week break between
  - Cohort retro and improvement
  - Batching cohorts (2x/year vs monthly)
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Dreading live sessions
  - Resentment building
  - Quality dropping
  - Considering quitting teaching
### **Detection Pattern**
exhausted|too much|burning out|need a break

## Timezone Disasters

### **Id**
timezone-disasters
### **Summary**
Global audience can't attend live sessions
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Major portions of audience excluded by timing
### **Why**
  Single timezone focus.
  Didn't consider global reach.
  Can't please everyone.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Timezone-Friendly Design
  
  ### Options
  | Approach | Trade-off |
  |----------|-----------|
  | Rotate times | Everyone inconvenienced equally |
  | Two sessions | More instructor time |
  | Regional cohorts | Smaller groups |
  | Async-first | Loses some live magic |
  
  ### Async-Heavy Model
  - 80% async content
  - 1 live session/week (rotates)
  - Recorded and shared quickly
  - Community for always-on support
  
  ### If Keeping Single Time
  - Be explicit in marketing
  - Show timezone clearly
  - Offer recording + notes
  - Create regional peer groups
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Complaints about timing
  - Large groups never attending
  - Refund requests citing timezone
  - Low engagement from regions
### **Detection Pattern**
timezone|too early|too late|can't attend|3am
