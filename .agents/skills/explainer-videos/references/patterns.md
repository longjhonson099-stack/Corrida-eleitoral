# Explainer Videos

## Patterns


---
  #### **Name**
The 90-Second Rule
  #### **Description**
Optimal explainer length for conversion and retention
  #### **When**
Determining explainer video length for landing pages or sales
  #### **Example**
    Data-backed structure:
    - 0-10 sec: Hook (problem statement that resonates)
    - 10-40 sec: Problem expansion (pain points, failed solutions)
    - 40-70 sec: Solution introduction (your product as hero)
    - 70-85 sec: How it works (3 simple steps)
    - 85-90 sec: CTA (clear next action)
    
    Why 90 seconds? Retention drops 20% after 90 seconds.
    Landing page explainers above 2 min see 40% lower play rates.
    

---
  #### **Name**
The Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS) Script
  #### **Description**
Classic copywriting framework adapted for video
  #### **When**
Scripting explainer videos for conversion
  #### **Example**
    Problem (first 15 seconds):
    "Managing customer data across 10 different tools is a nightmare."
    
    Agitate (next 20 seconds):
    "Your team wastes 5 hours a week on manual data entry. Important leads
    slip through the cracks. You can't get a single view of your customer."
    
    Solution (remaining time):
    "DataSync connects all your tools and gives you one source of truth..."
    
    The agitate phase is where most explainers fail. Go deeper on pain.
    

---
  #### **Name**
The Visual Metaphor
  #### **Description**
Use familiar concepts to explain unfamiliar ones
  #### **When**
Explaining abstract or technical concepts visually
  #### **Example**
    Abstract concept → Visual metaphor:
    - API → "A waiter taking your order to the kitchen and bringing back food"
    - Cloud computing → "Renting an apartment vs. buying a house"
    - Encryption → "A lock box that only your key can open"
    - Machine learning → "A child learning to recognize dogs vs. cats"
    
    Rule: The metaphor should be more familiar than the concept.
    Test: Would a 12-year-old understand the metaphor?
    

---
  #### **Name**
The Three-Step Framework
  #### **Description**
Break any complex process into exactly three steps
  #### **When**
Explaining how a product or process works
  #### **Example**
    Why three? Cognitive load research shows 3 is the magic number.
    
    Before: "First, create an account. Then verify your email. Next,
    set up your profile. After that, connect your integrations. Then..."
    
    After: "Three simple steps:
    1. Connect (add your tools in 30 seconds)
    2. Sync (watch your data flow together)
    3. Grow (make decisions with complete visibility)"
    
    Collapse complexity into three memorable steps.
    

---
  #### **Name**
The Show-Tell-Show Pattern
  #### **Description**
Layer visual and verbal information for maximum retention
  #### **When**
Explaining any concept that benefits from visual support
  #### **Example**
    Show (visual-first): Display the interface or process visually
    Tell (voice-over): Explain what they're seeing and why it matters
    Show (reinforcement): Visual confirmation of the benefit
    
    Bad: Voice says "click here" while showing unrelated animation
    Good: Voice says "click to export" while cursor clicks export button
    
    Visual and verbal must work together, not compete.
    

---
  #### **Name**
The Curse of Knowledge Antidote
  #### **Description**
Combat expert blindness by testing with novices
  #### **When**
Creating explainer content for complex products
  #### **Example**
    The curse: You've forgotten what it's like to not understand.
    
    Antidote process:
    1. Write script using simplest possible language
    2. Remove all jargon (yes, ALL of it)
    3. Test with someone outside your industry
    4. Note every question they ask
    5. Revise to answer those questions preemptively
    6. Repeat until zero questions
    
    If mom can't explain what your product does after watching,
    the video failed.
    

---
  #### **Name**
AI Explainer Video Production
  #### **Description**
Creating professional explainer videos with AI tools
  #### **When**
Need to produce explainer videos quickly and cost-effectively
  #### **Example**
    AI EXPLAINER VIDEO PRODUCTION:
    
    STYLE OPTIONS:
    
    1. AI AVATAR EXPLAINER (HeyGen/Synthesia)
       Best for: Product demos, training, updates
       Cost: ~$30-100 per video
       Time: 1-2 hours
    
       Workflow:
       - Script → AI voice → Avatar video → Edit
       - Add screen recordings as needed
       - Overlay graphics and captions
    
    2. AI ANIMATED EXPLAINER (Vyond + AI)
       Best for: Concept explanation, onboarding
       Cost: ~$50-200 per video
       Time: 2-4 hours
    
       Workflow:
       - Script → Vyond scenes → AI voice → Combine
       - Character animation with templates
       - Motion graphics for data/concepts
    
    3. AI GENERATED B-ROLL EXPLAINER
       Best for: Marketing, brand videos
       Cost: ~$100-300 per video
       Time: 4-8 hours
    
       Workflow:
       - Script → Human VO → AI B-roll (Veo3/Runway)
       - Mix AI visuals with screen captures
       - Professional editing and color
    
    SCRIPT STRUCTURE (for any style):
    0:00-0:05 - Hook (problem or intrigue)
    0:05-0:30 - Problem (pain point, status quo)
    0:30-1:30 - Solution (your product/concept)
    1:30-2:00 - How it works (demo/walkthrough)
    2:00-2:15 - Proof (results, testimonials)
    2:15-2:30 - CTA (clear next step)
    
    AI SCRIPT PROMPT:
    "Write a 2-minute explainer video script for [product].
    Target audience: [who]
    Key benefit: [main value]
    Tone: [professional/friendly/energetic]
    Include: hook, problem, solution, demo, proof, CTA"
    

## Anti-Patterns


---
  #### **Name**
Feature Listing
  #### **Description**
Walking through features instead of telling a story
  #### **Why**
Features don't create emotional connection; benefits and stories do
  #### **Instead**
Start with the problem, show transformation, then mention features in context

---
  #### **Name**
Jargon Overload
  #### **Description**
Using industry terminology that excludes newcomers
  #### **Why**
The people who need explainers most are those least familiar with jargon
  #### **Instead**
Use plain language. "Cloud-based SaaS solution" → "Software that works anywhere"

---
  #### **Name**
Solution Before Problem
  #### **Description**
Jumping straight to your product without establishing need
  #### **Why**
Viewers don't care about your solution until they feel the problem
  #### **Instead**
Spend first 30% on problem. Make viewers nod along before offering solution.

---
  #### **Name**
Information Dumping
  #### **Description**
Trying to explain everything in one video
  #### **Why**
Overwhelmed viewers remember nothing; focused viewers remember everything
  #### **Instead**
One video, one message. Create a series for complex topics.

---
  #### **Name**
Animation for Animation's Sake
  #### **Description**
Adding motion that doesn't support the message
  #### **Why**
Unnecessary animation distracts from the story and inflates production cost
  #### **Instead**
Every animation should clarify, not decorate. When in doubt, simplify.

---
  #### **Name**
Forgetting the CTA
  #### **Description**
Creating a great explainer but not telling viewers what to do next
  #### **Why**
Confused viewers take no action; clear direction converts
  #### **Instead**
End with single, clear CTA. "Start your free trial" not "Learn more, sign up, or contact us"
