# Competitive Intelligence - Sharp Edges

## Analysis Paralysis

### **Id**
analysis-paralysis
### **Summary**
Over-researching, under-acting
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Comprehensive competitive knowledge, no action
### **Why**
  Research is safer than action.
  Always more to learn.
  Fear of making wrong move.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Action-Oriented Intelligence
  
  ### Research Sufficiency
  
  ```
  You have enough research when:
  - Key competitors profiled
  - Main differentiators understood
  - Win/loss patterns identified
  - Action implications clear
  
  Don't need:
  - Every detail
  - Complete certainty
  - Perfect information
  ```
  
  ### Time-Boxed Research
  
  | Research Type | Time Box |
  |---------------|----------|
  | New competitor profile | 4 hours |
  | Quarterly refresh | 2 hours per competitor |
  | Battlecard update | 2 hours |
  | Win/loss analysis | 1 hour per interview |
  
  ### Action Requirements
  
  ```
  Every research effort must answer:
  - What do we DO differently?
  - Who needs to know?
  - When will we act?
  
  Research without action = waste.
  ```
  
  ### Decision Triggers
  
  | Signal | Action |
  |--------|--------|
  | Competitor price change | Review positioning |
  | Major feature launch | Battlecard update |
  | Strategic shift | Strategy review |
  | Multiple losses | Urgent response |
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Extensive competitive files, no changes
  - We need more research
  - Paralysis when competitor moves
### **Detection Pattern**
more research|understand better|comprehensive analysis

## Stale Intelligence

### **Id**
stale-intelligence
### **Summary**
Acting on outdated competitive information
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Competitive knowledge is months old
### **Why**
  Research was one-time effort.
  No refresh cadence.
  Market changed.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Intelligence Freshness
  
  ### Refresh Cadence
  
  | Element | Refresh | Owner |
  |---------|---------|-------|
  | Pricing | Monthly | Product Marketing |
  | Features | Monthly | Product |
  | Positioning | Quarterly | Marketing |
  | Strategy | Quarterly | Strategy |
  | Battlecards | Quarterly | Sales Enablement |
  
  ### Freshness Indicators
  
  ```
  Stale if:
  - Last update > 3 months
  - Major product launch not reflected
  - Pricing not verified recently
  - Win/loss not incorporated
  ```
  
  ### Automated Monitoring
  
  | Tool | Monitors |
  |------|----------|
  | Google Alerts | News mentions |
  | Job board tracking | Hiring priorities |
  | G2/Capterra | Reviews and ratings |
  | BuiltWith | Technology changes |
  | LinkedIn | People movements |
  
  ### Verification Habit
  
  ```
  Before using competitive info:
  - When was this last verified?
  - Has anything changed recently?
  - Is this still accurate?
  ```
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Surprised by competitor moves
  - Sales reporting different reality
  - Outdated battlecards
### **Detection Pattern**
when was this updated|is this current

## Competitor Following

### **Id**
competitor-following
### **Summary**
Following instead of leading
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Always reacting, never setting agenda
### **Why**
  Easier to copy than innovate.
  Fear of being wrong.
  Competitor validation feels safer.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Leading vs Following
  
  ### Follower Signals
  
  | Signal | Problem |
  |--------|---------|
  | "They launched X, we need X" | Reactive roadmap |
  | "They're doing Y, we should too" | Copy strategy |
  | "Wait and see what they do" | No conviction |
  
  ### Leader Mindset
  
  ```
  Ask:
  - What do customers need that nobody provides?
  - What's our unique insight?
  - What can we do that they can't?
  
  Act on these, not competitor moves.
  ```
  
  ### Strategic Choices
  
  | Approach | When |
  |----------|------|
  | Lead | You have unique insight |
  | Fast follow | They validated something |
  | Ignore | Doesn't fit your strategy |
  | Compete | You can win head-to-head |
  
  ### Setting the Agenda
  
  ```
  Leaders:
  - Define new categories
  - Set customer expectations
  - Make competitors react
  
  How:
  - Thought leadership
  - Innovation investment
  - Customer obsession
  ```
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Roadmap driven by competitor features
  - Always announcing "our version of X"
  - No unique innovation
### **Detection Pattern**
they have|they launched|competitor doing

## Fud Reliance

### **Id**
fud-reliance
### **Summary**
Selling against competitors instead of for yourself
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Sales leads with competitor criticism
### **Why**
  Easier than articulating own value.
  Feels like competitive edge.
  Sometimes works short-term.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Value-First Selling
  
  ### FUD Risks
  
  | Risk | Consequence |
  |------|-------------|
  | Credibility loss | Looks desperate |
  | Backlash | Customer defends competitor |
  | Accuracy | Claims get checked |
  | Focus | Conversation about them, not you |
  
  ### Value-First Structure
  
  ```
  Lead with:
  1. Their problem (understand deeply)
  2. Your solution (how you solve)
  3. Your proof (why believe you)
  
  Only then:
  4. Differentiation (why you vs others)
  
  Never lead with competition.
  ```
  
  ### Competitive Mentions
  
  | Situation | Approach |
  |-----------|----------|
  | They ask | Honest, balanced comparison |
  | You know they're evaluating | Landmine questions |
  | They mention strength | Acknowledge, pivot to your strength |
  | Direct comparison | Facts only, no FUD |
  
  ### Winning Without Negativity
  
  ```
  "I can't speak to their roadmap, but here's ours..."
  "I've heard good things about them for X. We focus on Y..."
  "Some customers choose them for [reason]. Others prefer us for..."
  ```
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Sales decks full of competitor criticism
  - Customers feel attacked
  - Win rate not improving
### **Detection Pattern**
competitor problems|why they're bad|attack
