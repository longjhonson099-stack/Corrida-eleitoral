# Channel Partnerships - Sharp Edges

## Dead Partner Problem

### **Id**
dead-partner-problem
### **Summary**
Signed partners that never produce revenue
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Partner count high, partner revenue low
### **Why**
  Signed without activation plan.
  No accountability for production.
  Recruitment without enablement.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Activating Dead Partners
  
  ### Diagnosis
  
  **Partner Health Audit**
  ```
  Active: Revenue in last 90 days
  Engaged: Activity in last 90 days (training, portal, etc.)
  Dormant: No activity in 90+ days
  Dead: No activity in 180+ days
  
  Healthy program: >50% active
  Problem program: <30% active
  ```
  
  ### Reactivation Playbook
  
  **Tier 1: High Potential Dormant**
  1. Executive outreach
  2. Understand barriers
  3. Dedicated success manager
  4. Co-investment offer
  
  **Tier 2: Medium Potential**
  1. Automated outreach campaign
  2. Offer incentive/SPIF
  3. Invite to partner event
  4. Provide qualified leads
  
  **Tier 3: Low Potential**
  1. Final notice
  2. Downgrade tier
  3. Consider termination
  4. Free up resources
  
  ### Prevention
  
  **Activation Requirements**
  ```
  Partner not "signed" until:
  - Training complete (30 days)
  - First deal registered (60 days)
  - First deal closed (90 days)
  
  Miss deadlines → revert to prospect
  ```
  
  **Partner Onboarding SLA**
  | Milestone | Days | Owner |
  |-----------|------|-------|
  | Portal access | 1 | Ops |
  | Training started | 7 | Partner |
  | Training complete | 30 | Partner |
  | First call scheduled | 14 | Channel mgr |
  | First deal registered | 60 | Partner |
  
### **Symptoms**
  - High partner count, low revenue
  - <30% of partners produced revenue this year
  - Partners complaining they get no support
### **Detection Pattern**
partner activation|dormant|inactive

## Channel Conflict Chaos

### **Id**
channel-conflict-chaos
### **Summary**
Direct and channel competing for same deals
### **Severity**
high
### **Situation**
Internal sales and partners fighting over accounts
### **Why**
  No clear rules of engagement.
  Deal registration not enforced.
  Incentives misaligned.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Channel Conflict Resolution
  
  ### Root Cause Analysis
  
  | Conflict Type | Cause | Solution |
  |---------------|-------|----------|
  | Price undercutting | No price parity | Enforce price rules |
  | Account overlap | No territory rules | Clear account assignment |
  | Deal stealing | No deal reg | Enforce registration |
  | Services conflict | Unclear scope | Define partner vs direct |
  
  ### Rules of Engagement
  
  **Account Assignment**
  ```
  Named accounts: Direct owns
  Territory accounts: First to engage owns
  Partner-sourced: Partner owns
  Inbound leads: Route by criteria
  ```
  
  **Deal Registration**
  ```
  Valid registration:
  - Submitted before customer contact
  - Customer confirms partner relationship
  - Protection period: 90 days
  - Renewable once
  
  Conflict resolution:
  - First valid registration wins
  - Customer preference breaks ties
  - Channel chief final arbiter
  ```
  
  **Price Parity**
  ```
  Partner price ≥ Direct price - Partner margin
  
  Example:
  Direct price: $10,000
  Partner margin: 20%
  Partner minimum price: $10,000 × 0.8 = $8,000
  
  No undercutting below partner floor
  ```
  
  ### Conflict Escalation
  
  | Level | Resolution Time | Arbiter |
  |-------|-----------------|---------|
  | 1 | 24 hours | Channel manager |
  | 2 | 48 hours | Channel director |
  | 3 | 72 hours | CRO/Channel chief |
  
  ### Compensation Alignment
  
  ```
  Direct rep on partner deal:
  - Full quota credit
  - Reduced commission (50%)
  - Encourage partner support
  
  Partner on direct deal:
  - Referral fee only
  - No margin
  - Clear expectations
  ```
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Sales and partners both claiming deals
  - Customers getting multiple quotes
  - Partners threatening to leave
### **Detection Pattern**
conflict|overlap|deal registration

## Over Reliance Single Partner

### **Id**
over-reliance-single-partner
### **Summary**
Too much revenue from one partner
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
One partner drives majority of partner revenue
### **Why**
  Easier to work with known partner.
  Others not developed.
  Single point of failure.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Partner Concentration Risk
  
  ### Risk Assessment
  
  | Concentration | Risk Level | Action |
  |---------------|------------|--------|
  | Top partner <30% | Low | Monitor |
  | Top partner 30-50% | Medium | Diversify |
  | Top partner >50% | High | Urgent diversify |
  
  ### Diversification Strategies
  
  **1. Develop Next Tier**
  ```
  Identify 3-5 partners with potential
  Invest disproportionately:
  - Dedicated success manager
  - MDF allocation
  - Lead sharing
  - Co-marketing
  
  Goal: Get 5 partners to 10%+ each
  ```
  
  **2. Vertical Specialization**
  ```
  Different partners for different verticals:
  - Healthcare partner
  - Financial services partner
  - Manufacturing partner
  
  Each can grow without competing
  ```
  
  **3. Geographic Diversification**
  ```
  Different partners for different regions:
  - North America
  - EMEA
  - APAC
  
  Natural separation, less concentration
  ```
  
  ### Relationship Risk Mitigation
  
  **With dominant partner:**
  - Deeper relationship (harder to leave)
  - Multiple contacts (not single thread)
  - Joint investments
  - Executive alignment
  - Contract terms (minimums, exclusivity)
  
  ### Contingency Planning
  
  ```
  If top partner left:
  - How much revenue at risk?
  - Which customers affected?
  - Who would take over?
  - How fast could we recover?
  
  Test: Could we survive 90 days?
  ```
  
### **Symptoms**
  - One partner >40% of channel revenue
  - Anxiety when partner relationship wobbles
  - No clear backup for key partner
### **Detection Pattern**
top partner|concentration|dependence

## Margin Erosion Trap

### **Id**
margin-erosion-trap
### **Summary**
Partner margins eating into profitability
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Channel revenue growing but contribution margin shrinking
### **Why**
  Margins set without total cost view.
  Partners demanding more over time.
  No margin governance.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Channel Economics Management
  
  ### Total Cost of Channel
  
  ```
  True channel cost:
  - Partner margin/discount
  - Deal registration incentives
  - MDF/Co-marketing
  - Partner management team
  - Training and enablement
  - Portal and tools
  - Events and travel
  
  Compare to: Direct sales cost
  ```
  
  ### Margin Architecture
  
  **Base Margin by Partner Type**
  
  | Type | Base Margin | Rationale |
  |------|-------------|-----------|
  | Referral | 10-15% | Lead gen only |
  | Reseller | 15-25% | Transactional |
  | VAR | 20-30% | Value-add |
  | SI | 25-35% | Major implementation |
  
  **Margin Modifiers**
  
  | Modifier | Impact | Criteria |
  |----------|--------|----------|
  | New logo | +5% | First deal with customer |
  | Strategic product | +5% | Pushing new product |
  | Large deal | +5% | >$100K |
  | Renewal | -5% | Lower effort |
  | Partner-sourced | +5% | They found it |
  
  ### Margin Governance
  
  **Quarterly Review**
  - Blended margin trending
  - Cost to serve analysis
  - Partner profitability ranking
  - Margin exception tracking
  
  **Discount Authority**
  | Discount Level | Approver |
  |----------------|----------|
  | Standard | Automatic |
  | +5% | Channel manager |
  | +10% | Channel director |
  | +15% | VP/CRO |
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Channel revenue up, margin down
  - Partners always asking for more
  - Deals less profitable than direct
### **Detection Pattern**
margin|discount|profitability

## Enablement Underinvestment

### **Id**
enablement-underinvestment
### **Summary**
Partners fail because they're not enabled
### **Severity**
medium
### **Situation**
Partners want to sell but can't close deals
### **Why**
  Assumed partners would self-enable.
  Enablement is cost, not investment.
  No measure of enablement effectiveness.
  
### **Solution**
  ## Partner Enablement Investment
  
  ### Enablement Budget Benchmark
  
  ```
  Partner enablement budget:
  10-15% of partner revenue target
  
  Example:
  Target partner revenue: $10M
  Enablement budget: $1-1.5M
  
  Includes:
  - Partner success team
  - Training development
  - Portal/tools
  - Events
  - Content creation
  ```
  
  ### Enablement Correlation
  
  **Track and Prove**
  ```
  Correlate training with performance:
  
  Certified partners:
  - Win rate: 35%
  - Deal size: $45K avg
  
  Non-certified partners:
  - Win rate: 18%
  - Deal size: $28K avg
  
  Certification ROI: Clear
  ```
  
  ### Enablement Priority Matrix
  
  | Capability Gap | Impact | Investment |
  |----------------|--------|------------|
  | Can't demo | High | Demo training + environment |
  | Can't position | High | Sales training + battlecards |
  | Can't implement | Medium | Technical certification |
  | Can't support | Medium | Support access + training |
  
  ### Self-Serve vs High-Touch
  
  **Self-Serve Enablement**
  - On-demand training
  - Partner portal content
  - Automated communications
  - For: All partners
  
  **High-Touch Enablement**
  - Live training sessions
  - Dedicated partner success
  - Custom enablement plans
  - For: Top tier partners
  
### **Symptoms**
  - Partners lose deals on product knowledge
  - Training completion rates low
  - Partners asking same questions repeatedly
### **Detection Pattern**
training|enablement|certification
